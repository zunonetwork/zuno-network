# Zuno Network

**Improved AI Accuracy, Powered by Humans and Decentralized Compute**

**The Problem:** AI is a powerful tool — but it still makes mistakes.

AI hallucinations produce incorrect answers with confidence, creating real‑world risk for businesses, governments, and users who need accuracy.


**The Solution:** Zuno Network

Zuno Network uses a smartphone app (Android and iOS) 
that turns everyday people into AI trainers. By reviewing 
AI-generated answers — a process the industry calls 
**data labeling** — contributors help improve AI accuracy 
and earn **Zuno tokens** on the Solana blockchain as a 
reward for every task completed.

No experience needed. No cost to join. 
Anyone in the world can contribute and earn.

*Zuno tokens are earned as rewards for completing tasks 
and are not offered as a financial investment.*

---

## Why Zuno

 
- **Human-validated AI data** — Contributors review AI-generated answers via the Zuno app
- **Decentralised validation** — Validator Nodes review contributor responses, reach consensus, and ensure only verified data enters the training dataset
- **Community-driven** — Every token is earned through real work, not purchased or allocated in advance
- **Built on Solana** — Fast, transparent, low-cost reward distribution
- **Accuracy at scale** — Consensus-verified data trains more reliable AI over time
---

## How It Works

1. **Distribute** — AI training tasks are broken into small pieces and sent to the Zuno app
2. **Label** — Contributors select the most accurate AI response for each task
3. **Validate** — Validator Nodes review submissions, score quality, flag suspicious responses, and reach consensus on the correct answer
4. **Reward** — Completed tasks earn points (pre-mine) or ZUNO tokens (at launch) from the Community Treasury — for both contributors and Validator Node operators
5. **Improve** — Consensus-verified outputs feed back into AI training, raising accuracy over time

## Current Phase

**Phase 0 — Private Beta Testing**
The Zuno app is being tested privately. Contributor workflows, the task pipeline, and the Validator Node layer are being validated before the public pre-mine begins. During the pre-mine, contributors and Validator Node operators earn points (not tokens) — 1 task = 1 point, max 10 per day. Points convert to ZUNO at 1:1 via airdrop at launch.
